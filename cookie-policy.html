1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies include localStorage — a browser storage mechanism that works like a cookie but stores data client-side without expiry. Cookies and localStorage can be "first-party" (set by us) or "third-party" (set by external services).

3. Your Consent

On your first visit, a banner asks you to choose between:

  • "Accept all" — you consent to the loading of Google Fonts (typography), Google Analytics 4 (website analytics), and Microsoft Clarity (session recordings and heatmaps). These services may process your IP address and browser information on their servers.
  • "Essential only" — only strictly necessary localStorage is used. Google Fonts will still load (loaded at page render for typography), but Google Analytics 4 and Microsoft Clarity will not be loaded. Your preference is recorded and no analytics tracking takes place.

Your consent is stored in localStorage (key: hts_cookie_consent) and is valid until you clear your browser data or reset it using the button below.
