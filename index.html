The morning, step by step

What happens,
from morning to table

01 10:00 AM — Arrive

We meet at the villa

You arrive at our historic estate in San Miniato — the undisputed capital of Italy's white truffle. We'll be there: us, Levante, Ares, and the smell of the forest already in the air. No signs. No crowds. Just you and a small group of lucky guests.

02 10:30 AM — Walk

Into the forest

Through the olive grove and into our private woodland. Levante and Ares take off. Their noses go to work. You learn to read the signals: where truffles grow, why they grow there, how moisture and tree roots create an underground miracle.

03 11:00 AM — The Hunt

You dig. You find it.

One of them stops. You kneel down. Your hands go into the earth. Then it happens — the truffle comes up, and with it a scent you've never experienced: intense, earthy, alive. This is the moment our guests still talk about months later.

04 12:00 PM — The Lesson

The knowledge no guidebook gives you

How to clean a truffle without ruining it. How to store it for five days without losing a gram of fragrance. How to tell a real truffle from an industrially scented fake. Francesca teaches what she knows because she's known it since childhood.

05 1:00 PM — The Feast

At the table: the truffle you found

Back at the villa, Francesca takes over. Fresh truffle pasta grated at the table. Our own estate olive oil. Carefully selected Tuscan wine. A table set the way it's done at home — not in a restaurant. And the silence of the hills around you.

Who takes you there

Francesca

"I don't sell an experience. I let you into something I've always done."

Francesca was born and raised near San Miniato, surrounded by these forests. She spent her childhood learning to read her dogs before she learned to read books — truffle hunting passed down through generations, not taught in classrooms.

She's not a tour guide. She's a truffle hunter who also happens to cook with extraordinary instinct. In the morning she leads the hunt. At noon she returns to the villa kitchen and prepares a lunch that guests compare, year after year, to a Michelin-starred experience.

Common questions

Everything you need to know

What if we don't find any truffles? +

It hasn't happened yet — but if it did, the experience doesn't change. Francesca knows these forests better than she knows her own kitchen. Some days you find two truffles, other days twelve. The unpredictability is part of what makes it real.

Is this suitable for children? +

Yes, from age 6 upward. Children are usually the most excited during the hunt — Levante and Ares draw them in immediately. The lunch works for all ages. Just drop us a note before booking so we can adjust the menu if needed.

How do I get to San Miniato without a car? +

San Miniato is reachable by train from Florence (San Miniato-Fucecchio station, ~35 minutes). Private transfers are also available — contact Eleonora for a personalised quote tailored to your pick-up point and group size.

When is the best time to come? +

The prized white truffle (Tuber Magnatum Pico) is found October through January — San Miniato is Italy's capital for it. Black truffle (Tuber Melanosporum) runs December to April. Summer black truffle is available in warmer months. The experience is rewarding all year, but October and November are pure magic.

Can I buy truffles to take home? +

Yes. At the end of the experience you can purchase fresh truffles directly from Francesca — at fair prices, without tourist-shop markups. We'll also show you how to keep them fresh during your journey home.
